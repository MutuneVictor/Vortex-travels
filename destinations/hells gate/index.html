Hell's Gate National Park

About the Park

Hell's Gate National Park is a protected area in Kenya located south of Lake Naivasha. The park covers an area of 68.25 square kilometres and is known for its scenic landscape, towering cliffs, and diverse wildlife. The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including buffalo, zebras, giraffes, baboons, and more than 100 species of birds.

Hell's Gate National Park is named after a narrow break in the cliffs, once a tributary of a prehistoric lake that fed early humans in the Rift Valley. The park is popular with hikers and rock climbers due to its natural rock formations and diverse topography.
